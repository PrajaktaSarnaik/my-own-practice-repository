Mental Health Myths & Facts

Myth

"Mental health problems are rare."

Fact

1 in 5 adults experience mental illness each year. It’s more common than you think!

Myth

"People with mental illnesses can’t hold down jobs."

Fact

Many people with mental health conditions work successfully in various fields.

Myth

"Children don't experience mental health issues."

Fact

Children can experience mental health challenges, and early intervention is crucial.

Frequently Asked Questions

Mindfulness is the practice of being aware of your thoughts, feelings, and body sensations in the present moment, without judgment.

Mindfulness can help reduce stress, anxiety, and depression by helping you stay grounded and present. It encourages self-awareness and acceptance, leading to a more balanced state of mind.

Start by practicing mindfulness through simple breathing exercises, meditation, or mindful activities like walking or eating. You can find many guided meditations online or through apps.

Yes, mindfulness can benefit anyone. It is a skill that can be developed over time and can help people from all walks of life cope with stress, anxiety, and other challenges.
